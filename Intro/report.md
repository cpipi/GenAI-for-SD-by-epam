### Generative AI Applications Report

---

### 1. Coding
This is basically like having a robot buddy who codes with you. You can just tell it what to do in plain English, and it writes the code for you. It's awesome for all the boring parts, like writing tests or filling out simple functions. It can also help you find bugs or explain code that you don't understand.

* **Why it's good for business:** It makes developers code way faster. This means companies can get new features out much quicker. It can also help make the code better by catching mistakes.
* **The hard part:** The AI doesn't always see the whole project, just the little piece you're working on. So its code might not fit in right. And sometimes the code it writes just... isn't very good. It works, but it's slow or messy.
* **Weak points:** The scary thing is it might spit out code that's from someone else's project, which could be a big legal problem (like security holes or copying). Also, if you use it too much, you might forget how to code yourself lol.
* **Who makes it:** GitHub Copilot is the big one. Amazon CodeWhisperer is another.

### 2. Marketing and Ads
This is when AI writes all the marketing stuff for a company. It can write blog posts, Instagram captions, emails, and even make the pictures for ads. It can make like a thousand different versions of one ad to see which one works best, or even make an ad thatâ€™s super specific just for you.

* **Why it's good for business:** It's super cheap and fast. You don't have to pay a writer or an artist, the AI just does it. And because the ads are so personal, people are more likely to click on them and buy stuff.
* **The hard part:** It's really hard to make the AI sound like a real person or a specific brand. And a lot of the time, it just makes stuff up, like fake facts or details, which is super embarrassing for the company.
* **Weak points:** A lot of the content is just... boring. You can tell a robot wrote it. It has no soul. Plus, there's a bunch of weird legal stuff about who owns the art the AI makes.
* **Who makes it:** Jasper, Copy.ai. And Adobe Firefly for making the pictures.

### 3. Chatbots (But Good Ones)
You know those really bad chatbots on websites? This is the new version. These new AI chatbots can actually understand what you're saying and have a real conversation. They can look up your order or help you solve a problem without you needing to talk to a human.

* **Why it's good for business:** This saves companies a ton of money because they don't need as many support people. And customers are happier (sometimes) because they get an answer right away, 24/7, instead of waiting on hold.
* **The hard part:** The AI has to be connected to the company's real info, or it will just start making up answers. Like, it might promise you a refund that you can't actually get. That's bad.
* **Weak points:** If the AI is bad, it's *really* bad. It can get you stuck in a loop and make you even angrier than just waiting for a person. It's also really expensive and complicated to set up right.
* **Who makes it:** a lot of companies are building their own now.

### 4. Medicine
This one is pretty wild. Scientists are using AI to invent new medicines. Instead of spending years guessing and checking in a lab, the AI can design a new drug on the computer. It's also used to figure out what tiny things in our bodies (like proteins) look like, which is a huge deal for understanding diseases.

* **Why it's good for business:** It makes research and development (R&D) way faster. Finding a new drug usually takes like 10 years and billions of dollars. AI could cut that down a lot.
* **The hard part:** Just because the AI *designs* a drug on the computer doesn't mean it works in real life. You still have to go make it and test it in a lab, which is slow.
* **Weak points:** A lot of what the AI designs is just junk that can't be made or doesn't work. And sometimes scientists don't even know *why* the AI suggested something, it's just a "black box," so it's hard to trust.
* **Who makes it:** Google's AlphaFold is the most famous one for this.
